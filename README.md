### About me

I am an AI/ML and full-stack engineer based in India who ships real, working systems, not demos. I like problems that sit at the edge of "is this even possible": grounded AI that refuses to make up a number, agents that actually do the work instead of just talking about it, and models small enough to run on a phone with no server behind them.

Most of what I build, I build alone, from the research and the data pipeline all the way to the deployed UI. I care about correctness first, then speed, then how it feels to use. A lot of my projects run at roughly zero dollars a month on free tiers, because a good architecture should not need a big bill to prove it works.

- Meta PyTorch x Scaler OpenEnv Hackathon 2026 **Grand Finalist**
- Google **The Big Code** Top 1,500
- **2x IEEE published** (EAIC 2025)
- Currently going deep on agentic systems, edge and on-device AI, and retrieval that can be trusted

### What I like building

- **Agentic AI** that plans, fans out to specialists, and takes real actions instead of returning a wall of text
- **Edge and on-device AI** that keeps private data on the device and still feels intelligent
- **Grounded RAG** where every answer is traceable to a source and nothing is fabricated
- **Cinematic front ends** with real motion design, scroll choreography, and WebGL when the moment earns it
- **Research to product**, turning a paper or a dataset into something a person can actually open and use

### How I work

- **Solo and end to end.** Research, data, model, backend, and front end are all mine on most of these projects.
- **Grounded by default.** If a number appears, it came from a source. My systems are built to say "I do not know" instead of making something up.
- **Lean infrastructure.** Many of these run near zero dollars a month on free tiers, because the architecture does the heavy lifting.
- **Motion with meaning.** When I reach for animation, it is to guide attention, not to decorate.

### Beyond the code

Formula 1 is the reason [Apex](https://github.com/ShAuRyA-Noodle/Apex-F1) exists, part fan project and part excuse to build a hard real-time data system. Outside of that I read about how large systems fail, keep a running list of ideas worth building, and ship something most days of the week.
